Marine Biodiversity

What is Marine Biodiversity?

Marine Biodiversity refers to the variety of life forms found in the ocean and other saltwater environments like seas, coral reefs, and estuaries. It includes all living organisms in these ecosystems, from the smallest microscopic plankton to the largest whales.

Importance of

Marine Diversity

Marine biodiversity is important because it plays a vital role in maintaining the health and balance of ocean ecosystems. Oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and are home to a wide variety of species, from tiny plankton to large whales. These diverse marine species support life on Earth by producing oxygen, regulating the climate, and providing food and livelihoods for millions of people. Healthy marine ecosystems also protect coastlines from erosion and natural disasters like tsunamis and storms.
